## Overview

Year 1 ends with a secret in play: the weapon's target is the Keth homeworld, the Others' ruins are buried under living Keth civilization, and at least one faction now has coordinates. Years 2–5 are about what everyone does with that knowledge — who controls the discovery, who gets to decide what it means, and what it costs to find out the truth about a civilization that didn't survive to tell it themselves.

## Year 2 Beats (Detailed)

### Setup
Year 2 picks up in the aftermath of Year 1's ending. The weapon's broadcast was detected by all four factions. The station's situation is no longer containable.

### The Federation Arrives
The Federation's patience ran out at the end of Year 1 Act 3. Year 2 opens with Federation ships near Outpost Nova in force — not a patrol, not a routine check. They have identified the signal as Builder-origin and they know Hegemony got there first.

Internal Federation hawks and doves are at war. The doves want to negotiate; the hawks want to act. The specific shape of what "act" means depends on Year 1's ending.

### The Keth Coalition Forced Into the Open
The ruling coalition can no longer monitor silently. The weapon events — the broadcast, Harlan's visit, whatever the crew chose — have made the station a public intelligence priority. The coalition must respond through official channels, which means acknowledging that they've been watching, which means explaining why.

What they cannot explain without exposing the lie: why they had ancient-tech detection systems pointed at this region. The reckoning is arriving from inside the institution, not just from outside.

### Nadia's Arc
Nadia learns that she was an unwitting asset.

The specific mechanism: a Federation intelligence review following Year 1 reveals that the station's assignment was not routine. Nadia was sent to watch for a signal her handler described in terms she didn't recognize — the handler knew what they were looking for and didn't tell her. She finds out in Year 2.

The Hopeful / Detached / Curious profile: the Detachment axis was her armor for the transactional nature of the work. It cracks when she realizes the transaction was larger than she was told. The Hopeful axis is what she has to decide what to do with: keep believing in the Federation's stated mission, or reckon with the distance between the mission and the institution. This is not a fast arc.

The Curious axis stays intact — it's what drives her to investigate what she missed rather than look away.

**Nadia ↔ Vaen collision (Year 2):** Nadia realizes what Vaen is carrying and understands retroactively what she was sent to watch for. The uncanny mirror of Year 1 cracks — she's on one side of something Vaen is on the other side of, and neither of them chose it cleanly. Specific scene, to be written in Year 2 design.

### Weapon Critical (Defend Ending Only)
If the superintendent chose Ending 1 (Defend), the weapon reaches critical charge in Year 2 Act 3. What "critical" means — for the station, for the Keth homeworld, for the factions watching — is the Year 2 climax beat for that track.

### Vaen's Next Step
Depending on Year 1's ending, Vaen holds coordinates pointing to the Keth homeworld. The ship fund is not enough yet. Year 2 is about what Vaen does with knowledge they cannot act on alone, in a situation where everyone around them has reasons to want the knowledge contained.

## Year 3 Beats (Detailed)

### Unaligned Alliance Engages
The Alliance has been watching. By Year 3, the cost of staying silent outweighs the cost of engagement. They make formal contact — with the station, with the superintendent (depending on Year 1 outcome), and with the other factions. Their stated interest: ensuring no single faction controls the Builder discovery. Their actual interest: their own access to whatever leverage the discovery represents.

### Discovery Expedition
Secondary Others sites — accessible without Keth homeworld access — become the Year 3 expedition arc. Vaen's artifact serves as a partial guide. The Others' documentation at secondary sites begins to make the biological connection undeniable, even if the homeworld evidence is still locked.

The expedition is not clean. All four factions have interests in who finds what and in what order. The field conditions of Years 2–3 are contested, not collaborative.

### The Keth Society Fracture Deepens
The ruling coalition's attempt to contain the discovery is failing from inside. Navigator-caste Keth who dealt with humans during the Fracture are the fracture point — they have questions the Keeper caste cannot answer without unraveling the lie. By Year 3, some of them have started talking to people outside Keth official channels.

This is the pressure that makes Year 4's homeworld access possible: not a military or diplomatic victory, but the ruling coalition's internal authority cracking under questions it cannot answer.

### Velreth ↔ Vaen Arc
Vaen learns what happened to the Thessari — that their people's war caught Velreth's world in the crossfire, that neither side claimed it, that Velreth has been rebuilding warmth from scratch ever since. This is the Year 3 beat for that relationship. What Vaen does with that knowledge, and whether it changes anything between them, is the arc.

## Years 4–5 Beats (Broad — Deliberately Deferred)

### Year 4
- The ruling coalition's authority cracks. The mechanism is internal — questions the lie cannot survive.
- Homeworld access becomes possible. The Others' ruins are excavated from under living Keth civilization.
- The biological connection between the Keth and the Others is confirmed. All cultural memory was lost; the connection is entirely biological. What this means for Keth society, for the cosmological lie, for the factions watching — is the Year 4 arc.
- The expedition to the homeworld is staged alongside the political fracture.
