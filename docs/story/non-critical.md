# Non-Critical
### *A short story — Outpost Nova, Year 1*

---

The power draw in the lower corridor logged at 0612, fourteen minutes before the morning shift rotation.

Dex caught it on the secondary panel — not an alert, not an alarm, just a reading that didn't match the baseline. He pulled it up. The signature was wrong in a way that was hard to characterize: not a spike, not a drop, not anything that mapped to a specific system drawing more or less than it should. Just a shape he hadn't seen before in that part of the station.

He sat with it for a moment. Then annotated the entry — *lower-deck-adjacent, power signature inconsistent with baseline, no load source identified, non-critical* — and filed it.

He poured a second cup of something and went to work.

---

The authorization came through at 0900. Superintendent's stamp, routine maintenance order: ventilation routing check, lower corridor section 7-C. Quen listed as security oversight. Scheduled for 1100.

Dex read it on his board while he was running a diagnostic on the secondary coupling array. He had the anomaly file open in another window. He looked at the authorization, then at the file, then at the authorization again.

He closed the anomaly file and approved the routing.

---

At 1047 he watched Quen cross the engineering bay on the way to the lower corridor. They moved the way they always moved — fluid, unhurried, like something that had been navigating this station for longer than the station remembered. They didn't look his way.

Dex went back to the coupling array.

---

The second entry logged at 1314.

Same source. Different shape. The draw had shifted — not larger, not smaller, but different in a way that felt like something had happened in the interval. Like the system had been asked a question and had answered it.

He pulled both entries up side by side and looked at them for a while.

Then he added a note — *anomaly updated, pattern shift, previous entry attached, non-critical* — and filed it.

---

He didn't know when Quen came back up. He was under the deckplating at 1400, running cable in section four, and by the time he was out and cleaned up it was nearly 1600. He checked the corridor log. Quen had signed out of lower section 7-C at 1248.

He didn't look up where Quen had gone after that. He had a reasonable idea.

---

Velreth's medical bay was quiet in the mid-afternoon. It usually was.

Quen arrived at 1301 — nine minutes after signing out of the lower corridor, which was brisk. They presented with a laceration on the secondary left manipulator, moderate depth, clean edges. Maintenance hazard, structurally consistent with the work they'd been assigned.

Velreth cleaned and sealed the laceration. It took eleven minutes, which was longer than necessary, because they also ran a panel scan — a quiet one, a protocol they had built themselves and not submitted to the station's standard diagnostic suite, because there was no standard diagnostic suite for what it was looking for.

The result came back in three minutes.

*Unclassified emitter exposure, lower-deck-adjacent source. Case four.*

The first three cases had resolved within 72 hours. No lasting sequelae. Velreth had treated each one by the same protocol — radiation flush, anti-inflammatory, rest — without explaining to any of the patients what the protocol was for.

They applied it.

"The laceration will be fully healed in four days," they said. "Reduced grip load on that side in the meantime."

Quen's chromatophores were cycling slow and even, the way they did when Quen was managing something. "Understood," it said.

"The flush takes approximately twenty minutes. You can stay or return — the compound works the same either way."

"I'll stay," Quen said.

Velreth nodded and went to the cabinet.

They did not say: *I have three prior files with this exact signature. I know what you were standing near.* They did not say: *The first patient was twelve years ago, before the sealing. She didn't ask what I found, either.* They did not say anything about the lower decks, about what Quen had been near, about what the reading meant.

Quen sat on the examination table with its chromatophores cycling slow, and did not say: *I know you know.* Did not say anything about the weight of the reading, or what it felt like to stand in a corridor and feel something notice you. Did not say anything at all.

Velreth administered the flush and they waited out the twenty minutes, and the silence was the compact they had arrived at without negotiating, which was the only kind that held.

---

The superintendent found Dex at end of shift, doing the walkthrough.

"Lower corridor check came back clean," the superintendent said. "Anything unusual on your end today?"

Dex had the anomaly file on his tablet. Both entries. The shape of the second one, which was different from the first in a way that meant something he didn't have a name for.

"Nothing to report," he said.

The superintendent nodded and moved on.

---

He found Quen near the secondary docking port, which was where Quen often was in the late afternoon.

"Everything go all right down there?"

Quen's chromatophores were neutral — the careful cycling of something that had decided on its answer before the question arrived.

"Yes," it said.

"Good. Appreciate you taking it."

"Of course," Quen said.

---

The cantina was quieter than usual by the evening. Dex took a table near the far wall with a drink and his tablet, though he wasn't reading anything on the tablet. Maris moved at the counter, doing the late work. The station settled into its night sounds.

Velreth came in at 2130 and sat across from him without being asked, which was how they usually did it. Maris brought something over without being asked, which was how she usually did it.

They sat for a while.

"Power draw's been inconsistent," Dex said. He was looking at the table.

Velreth considered this. "The body compensates," they said, after a moment. "Usually without knowing it's doing so."

Dex picked up his drink.

Neither of them said anything else.

---

*Superintendent's Log — End of Day*

*Lower corridor ventilation check completed, section 7-C. No anomalies found. Station systems nominal. Maintenance backlog reduced by one item. Day rated non-critical.*

---

*End.*
