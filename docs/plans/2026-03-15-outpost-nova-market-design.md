# Outpost Nova — Market & Game Design

**Date:** 2026-03-15
**Type:** PC Indie Game (solo developer)

---

## Market Opportunity

The cozy game market (~$1B, growing 6.5%/yr) is underserved in one specific way: players want characters that feel real and building that feels meaningful. Stardew Valley proved the massive appetite for this genre but left both pillars shallow — NPCs are static, building is farming-centric.

**The gap:** A cozy settlement builder with dynamically reactive characters and a strong sense of legacy. No current title delivers both.

**Target audience:** 25-45, skews female, higher disposable income, seeks therapeutic but meaningful gameplay. Strong DLC and seasonal content willingness.

---

## Concept

You arrive as the founding administrator of a brand-new space station at the edge of explored space. Settlers trickle in — scientists, traders, drifters, dreamers. You build the station module by module while forming real relationships with the people who call it home.

**Working title:** Outpost Nova
**Platform:** PC (Steam / itch.io)
**Tone:** Hopeful & frontier — optimistic, "we're building something together"

---

## Core Loop

```
Build modules → Attract new settlers → Settlers bring stories & needs
→ Relationships deepen → Events shake the community
→ Station grows, reflects who you've become
```

---

## Core Pillars

### 1. Legacy Building
- Expand the station room by room: hydroponics bay, observatory, med bay, cantina, workshops
- Each module unlocks new settlers, events, and story threads
- The station visually evolves — aged panels, personal decorations, community murals
- After years (in-game), the station feels *lived in*, not just built

### 2. Characters Who Live
- Each settler has a backstory, a reason they came, and a personal arc
- Characters form relationships with each other independently
- Player choices (which modules to build, who to house together, resource allocation) influence — but don't script — their stories
- Characters can leave, return, change, or surprise you

### 3. Narrative Texture Without Cutscene Walls
- Story delivered through: letters, overheard conversations, seasonal station events, personal quests
- Players who dig get depth; casual players get charm
- Organic discovery over exposition dumps
