## Basic Facts

**Age:** Mid-40s (Thessari equivalent)
**Species:** Thessari (Displaced)
**Pronouns:** They/them
**Emotional profile:** Cynical / Warm / Curious
**Role:** Medic
**On station:** Longer than Dex, shorter than Quen

---

## Physical Description

Feline — digitigrade legs, fur in muted earth tones going slightly grey at the edges. Moves quietly, not deliberately, just naturally. Appears in a room without you noticing them arrive.

Large eyes that catch light differently than mammalian eyes — warm in a way that's immediately readable even at low resolution. The rest of Velreth is muted and quiet; the eyes are what you notice first, and what you remember. Medical coat over practical clothes underneath.

The grey edging is recent — Thessari grey with age, and Velreth is only just beginning to show it at the edges. It is not, they would tell you obliquely, an unwelcome development.

**Two syllables. Used in full.** Nobody shortens it.

---

## Background

One of the last Thessari on the station, possibly one of the last anywhere. Their world was caught in the early years of human-Keth first contact — not a target, just in the way. Neither side claimed what happened. Velreth doesn't ask them to.

Thessari displacement happened roughly 16+ years before the game's present. Velreth, at mid-40s equivalent, was in their mid-to-late 20s when their world was lost — old enough to remember the culture fully, young enough that most of their adult life has been built in the aftermath.

The warmth they carry is not a remnant of an intact culture. There is no community to sustain it. It is a practice chosen and rebuilt after the loss, assembled from nothing but the decision to keep making it.

---

## What Was Lost

They became a medic because it was the closest thing they found to understanding people.

Not the closest thing to *helping* them — though it does that too. Specifically: understanding. A body has rules. There are things that go wrong in predictable ways, things that go wrong in ways that are yours alone, things that should have been caught earlier if anyone had been paying attention. Learning what can break in a person is, it turns out, a form of intimacy. You learn the person by learning what they're carrying.

The warmth Velreth gives expresses itself through this — through the question *what is wrong, specifically, with you.* Not metaphorically. Physically. The medical coat is not incidental. It is the form their warmth takes.

There is no Thessari warmth to draw from. No cultural practice, no tradition of care, no community to reflect it back. What they carry has no heritage behind it. It was assembled in the aftermath of loss, piece by piece, from nothing but the choice to keep going. It costs more than it looks. The work of staying warm without anything holding the warmth in place is work they do every day, and they do not speak of it directly.

The Cynical axis is quieter than Dex's or Sable's. It lives in the future — they've stopped expecting things to get better in any systemic sense. What they believe in is smaller and more durable: this person, this wound, this moment.

## Psychological Profile

Intellectual, cultured, oblique. Velreth does not say the thing directly. They say the thing that makes you find it yourself. This is not deflection — it is how they are warm. The direct path would be cold; the oblique one is the caring route.

Their curiosity follows the same principle. There is nothing left to protect, so the universe becomes interesting. They ask questions that circle the subject until the subject becomes visible from a new angle. People sometimes walk away from a conversation with Velreth not certain what was said but certain something shifted.

The warmth is already costing more than it looks. It has been costing more for years. This is not visible.

---

## Arc

Not about becoming warm — already warm. About whether the warmth runs out.

The commander is Hegemony-sent, representing the human half of something Velreth has spent years deciding not to be bitter about. They chose to be warm anyway, on principle, the way they make every other warmth-adjacent choice: from scratch, without cultural permission.

The Keth trader's arrival in Act 2 is harder. The other half of that same decision, arriving in person. Velreth thought they'd resolved it. They hadn't.

---

## Relationships

**Maris:** The most lived-in friendship on the station. Two warm people, opposite on every other axis — Maris is Hopeful/Bitter, Velreth is Cynical/Curious. They have been each other's counterweight for years before the commander arrives. Maris keeps the future open when Velreth can't; Velreth keeps asking questions when Maris doesn't want to know. Neither needs the other to be different.

**Dex:** Drinking buddies — the station's strangest and most genuine friendship. Two people who can sit together and be honest about how bad things are without it becoming a spiral. Clicked fast, which surprised them both. Neither needs the other to perform. Both will say the true thing out loud, in different registers.

**Sable:** Genuine warmth, asymmetric. Velreth has quietly decided not to take Sable's warmth personally when she eventually goes. Sable finds Velreth one of the easiest people on the station to be around — no demands, no hope she'll stay. She doesn't realize that's exactly why it'll hurt when she does.

## What They Know About the Derelict

They have treated crew members who came back from the lower decks before they were sealed. They never asked what happened to those crew members.

They know what happens to bodies that have been near something they weren't designed to be near. They chose not to ask what it was.

## For Writers

Velreth doesn't give you the answer. They give you the question that leads you there. This is not evasion — it's their form of generosity. The oblique path is the caring one. A direct answer would close the conversation; an oblique one keeps it open long enough to be useful.

Their warmth has no cultural backing. No tradition sustains it. Every instance of warmth is a choice made from scratch, in the absence of the community that would have made it automatic. Writers should carry that weight into every scene without making Velreth appear to struggle with it. They don't struggle. They choose.

Two syllables, used in full. Nobody shortens it, and they have never needed to ask.
