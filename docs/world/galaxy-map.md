## How to Read This Map

The map shows a ~2,000 ly bubble of the Orion Arm centered on human expansion space.
Human settlement runs roughly east–west along the galactic arm. Keth Space sits
coreward (above the main human axis) — the Keth do not face humanity head-on; they
approach from above, perpendicular to the expansion axis.

**Symbol key:**

| Symbol | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| Filled circle | World or inhabited body |
| Circle with white stroke | Orbital station |
| Hexagon outline + center dot (amber) | Outpost Nova — Builder primary node |
| × (grey) | Destroyed world |
| ◇ (pale violet) | Builder secondary site (approximate, non-canonical) |

## Regions

### Federation Core (deep blue)
The heart of human Federal authority. Worlds here are older colonial settlements
absorbed into the Federation military-administrative apparatus during consolidation.
High infrastructure density. Population largely accepts Federation governance as
normal — the war confirmed their worldview, not challenged it.

### Inner Frontier (grey-purple)
The frontier where corporate extraction infrastructure preceded any real government.
Mixed human factions, non-human communities, legal ambiguity. The Federation's reach
extends here but frays at the edges. This is where the war was most complicated —
loyalties split along extraction debts, not ideology.

### Border Zone (dark amber)
Legally contested space. Unaligned Alliance worlds sit here — too isolated during the
Fracture to be worth conquering, too stubborn to be absorbed afterward. The emerging
Keth trade lane cuts through this zone, which makes it suddenly strategically
interesting to everyone who previously ignored it. Outpost Nova sits here.

### Fracture Zone (dark red, sub-region within Border Zone)
The epicenter of the Fracture conflict — where the war was most brutal and unresolved.
Named by Unaligned worlds; the Federation calls it the Border Stabilization Zone.
The Keth have no word for it in any human language. Thessara was here.

### Keth Space (deep green)
Keth sovereign territory. The external face shown to humans is controlled and
formal — merchant lanes, trade protocols, diplomatic channels. The internal reality
(caste fractures, cosmological crisis, the lie underpinning Keth society) is entirely
invisible from this map's vantage point.

### The Unmapped (dark, top and bottom)
Beyond the known-space bubble. Not empty — unmapped. The distinction matters:
the Keth homeworld (Vaethos) is technically at the edge of charted Keth space;
what lies beyond is unknown to either civilization. The weapon at Outpost Nova points
inward, not outward.

## Worlds and Stations

### Federation Core

**Earth (Sol)** — Federation capital. The political and symbolic center of human
authority. Most characters in the game have never been here and never will be.

**Harrow** — Military-industrial world. Dex's birth world. Commander (Engineer track)
birth world. The Federation's war machine drew heavily on Harrow's manufacturing base.

**Velan** — Administrative and cultural capital. Nadia's birth world. Commander (Medic
track) birth world. Produces the Federation's bureaucrats, journalists, and diplomats.

### Inner Frontier

**Breck** — Rough extraction colony. Maris and Harlan's birth world — a coincidence
neither of them knows yet. Exactly the kind of place that ended up on the wrong side
of a Hegemony debt.

**Auren** — Ishari Compact territory. Strategic trade junction between human space and
the emerging Keth lane. The Ishari Compact has been quietly building leverage here for
fifteen years; post-truce, it's paying off.

**Surev Prime** — Capital of the Surev Reaches. Holds the only intact pre-Keth-contact
astronomical charts predating human arrival in the Frontier by 200+ years. The
Federation, Keth Merchants, and Hegemony are all courting the Directorate simultaneously.

**Vaedra** — Drueth homeworld. Largely abandoned. Hegemony's extraction operations
disrupted the land systems the Drueth kinship networks depended on — not in a single
dramatic event, but slowly, before anyone was paying attention. The displacement was
finished before the Fracture started.

**Ashfeld** — Drueth refugee settlement. Commander (Drifter track) birth world. What
passes for community when the original structure is gone.

**Carnach Station** — Hegemony Combine HQ orbital. Sits at the Inner Frontier / Border
Zone boundary, technically in Unaligned space — Hegemony's legal team designed it that
way. The hub of Hegemony's Extraction Web trade lanes. Every debt in the Frontier has
a line that eventually leads here.

*Hegemony Extraction Web nodes (◉ orange, Inner Frontier):*

**Relay Corriv** — Northern relay station in the Inner Frontier. Routes extraction
traffic from the northern reaches down toward Carnach. The Ishari on Auren tolerate
its presence because Hegemony pays transit fees; they don't tolerate much else.

**Outpost Vael** — The furthest north Hegemony has permanent infrastructure in the
Frontier. Positioned to monitor the emerging Keth trade lane before Hegemony had
official access to it. Still does.

**Depot Ashkell** — Western logistics depot. Processes raw extraction output from
the outer Inner Frontier before forwarding to Carnach. Sits uncomfortably close to
Federation space — which is, again, by Hegemony's legal team's design.

**Node Marek** — Southern distribution node. Coordinates outbound debt-shipments
to Surev Prime and Breck. The workers here know where the goods go; they stopped
asking where they came from years ago.

**Platform Dunn** — Eastern platform at the Inner Frontier / Border Zone edge.
The closest Hegemony infrastructure to Outpost Nova outside Carnach Station itself.
Its existence is one reason the station's "neutral" status is contested.

### Border Zone

**Sundra** — Unaligned Alliance core anchor world. Sable's adopted home — blockaded
during the Fracture. The Alliance considers its survival vindication. Sundra is where
"not our war" was held longest and at greatest cost.

**Outpost Nova** *(ring/hex, amber)* — The game location. A Hegemony-operated waypoint
station built on top of a buried Builder primary node. The weapon inside the station is
aimed at Vaethos. The station's legal ambiguity, Keth trade lane proximity, and
extraordinary underlying structure make it the intersection point for every faction's
agenda. See `docs/story/year1.md`.

### Fracture Zone

**Thessara** *(× symbol)* — Soriel's birth world. Destroyed in the early years of
human-Keth first contact — not a target, just in the way. Neither side claimed it.
Neither side has been asked to. The cross symbol marks a world that no longer exists.
Soriel is one of the last fluent speakers of the language all Thessari names draw from.

### Keth Space

**Vaethos** — Keth homeworld. Where the weapon at Outpost Nova points. The Others'
ruins are buried under living Keth civilization — the ruling coalition suspects this;
the lower castes do not. The weapon has been aimed here since before the Keth existed
as a civilization.

**Korath** — Keth Merchant caste hub. Vaen's birth world. The commercial face the
Keth show to other species. Inner leadership here knows significantly more than it
discloses.

## Trade Lanes

### Federation Spine (solid blue)
The main east-west artery of human commerce and military transit. Old infrastructure,
reliable, heavily patrolled. The Federation's presence on this lane is not subtle.

### Keth Internal Routes (solid green, within Keth Space)
Keth-internal lanes not open to human traffic. Shown here for geographic context only.

### Emerging Keth Trade Lane (dashed green)
The lane that makes Outpost Nova strategically relevant. Runs diagonally from Keth
Space through Auren (Ishari junction), then east through the Border Zone to Outpost
Nova. Five years old. Growing. Every faction has an opinion about who should control it.

### Hegemony Extraction Web (dotted amber)
Hegemony's informal supply and debt network threading through the Inner Frontier with
Carnach Station as hub. Not a shipping lane so much as a financial architecture made
visible. The dotted style is intentional — this network operates in the gaps between
official routes.

### Unaligned Passage Routes (dotted teal)
Informal transit lanes connecting Sundra and scattered Border Zone worlds. How the
Unaligned Alliance moves without using Federation or Hegemony infrastructure. Lightly
marked, deliberately. Being visible on this lane is not always an asset.

## Builder Sites

**Primary node:** Outpost Nova. The weapon is here.

**Secondary sites (◇, approximate):** The Keth encountered Builder infrastructure at
secondary relay nodes and processing stations during early Keth space exploration.
They reverse-engineered what they could and built their sacred cosmology on top of the
geometric patterns — projecting meaning onto shapes whose origin they never understood.
The diamond markers on this map are approximate; their canonical positions are not
locked. *See `docs/world/world.md` §1.1 for full Builder civilization context.*

## Regenerating This Map

```bash
python3 docs/world/generate_map.py
```

Coordinate and data tables are at the top of the script. To adjust a world's position,
edit its `(x, y)` values in the `WORLDS` dict. To add a world, add an entry with the
appropriate `symbol` and `region` keys. Re-run the script and commit both files.
